A Comprehensive Analysis of the Effects of Sleep on Human Body----From the Perspectives of Logit Model and Machine Learning

Abstract

By using Logit Model, we successfully found out first three factors that influence the Sleep Quality most, which are Sex, Age and Nervousness and build up a quantitative model among them. By applying Lasso, Ridge and their improved models, we were able to extract the key features that have great impact on the diagnosis results. We found that the diagnosis results are closely related with seven features. Each of them are scored under different models and we made a Sleep Index to represent the general condition of sleep. With the comparison of different classification model, we successfully select the best classier with its features. We found that Decision Tree Classifier with seven features has the highest prediction accuracy at 84.5%. Finally, by using the Sleep Index, we try to construct a model to optimize the best sleep time for us to main healthy.

Introduction

China has become the second largest economy in the world in recent years and our people is embracing a better life today. However, the competition in job markets is becoming much fiercer as well. We could see that there is an increasing number of people suffering from stress and insomnia, resulting in poor health. As the living condition continue to go up, nowadays, people are more concerned about their health than ever before. And health is closely connected with sleep, it if necessary for us to analyze the relationship between sleep and health. In this essay, by using logit model and other machine learning method, we try to develop a comprehensive model between sleep and health, which could help people to main a balance between rest and work.
Considering the fact that all the data given is discrete, as Williams (2006) pointed out that using logit model could be more robust, we use the logit model to find out the relationship between the indicators given and the sleep quality. After that, we apply different machine learning models to exact the key features of sleep and construct a Sleep Index. Later we used different classification models to train “AI doctors”, and we select the best of them, which is the Decision Tree Classifier. Finally, by using the Sleep Index, we try to construct a model to optimize the best sleep time for us to main healthy.

Problem One: What is the relationship between the indicators given and the quality of sleep?

Data Clean > Correlation Analysis > Variable list > Logit Model > Conclusion

In a nutshell, the first three factors that influence the Sleep Quality are Sex, Age and Nervousness, which is quite conceivable. Nowadays, generally, male would take more responsibility in earning sufficient funds to raise the family, and suffer more competition with others. Therefore, it is conceivable that male have a higher probability of facing a worse sleep quality. And in our daily life, the old suffer from egersis more often. And it is quite obvious that the more nervous one is, the less likely that he is going to have good sleep quality.

Problem Two: What is the relationship between the diagnosis results and sleep?

Feature Extraction > Feature Score, Model Regression Results and Analysis > Further Analysis > Sleep Index > Conclusion

By analyzing the scatter point and the feature selection model results, we could drop two features Sex and Hypnagogue. The reason why we don’t take Sex into account is that the spread of diseases in male and female are almost the same which means that Sex is a partial unrelated factor. The reason why we could delete Hypnagogue is because it scores the lowest in each model

Problem Three: Assuming you are a doctor, what diagnosis would you make to the patient based on the data in Annex III?

Classification Models > Conclusion

From Fig. 12 to Fig.17, we could see that generally, the accuracy rate of RandomForest Classifier
Decision Tree Classifier would increase as the number of features increase. The best classification feature set includes all the seven features and the best classifier is the Decision Tree, which could reach an accuracy rate of 84.5%.
Therefore, we decides to use the Decision Tree Classifier with seven features to do the diagnosis prediction.
